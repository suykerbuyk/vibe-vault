# Competitive Landscape Analysis — Reference

## Core Frameworks

### 1. Competitive Category Mapping
Always map three layers:
- **Direct competitors:** Same product, same buyer, same use case
- **Adjacent competitors:** Different product but competing for the same budget line or displacing the same workflow
- **Substitute behaviors:** What do buyers do today without any vendor? (Excel, manual process, consultant, do nothing)

The "do nothing" option is always a competitor and often the most dangerous one in regulated industries where inertia is high.

### 2. Moat Assessment Matrix

| Moat Type | Description | Startup Viability | Questions to Ask |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Network effects** | Product improves as more users join | Strong if defensible | Is value proportional to nodes, or does one large player capture it? |
| **Data moat** | Proprietary data that improves over time | Strong if exclusive | Is data truly exclusive, or can incumbents acquire it? |
| **Switching costs** | High cost to migrate away | Moderate | How long before lock-in occurs? What triggers migration? |
| **IP/patents** | Protected technology | Weak in software alone | Is this patentable? Does patent deter well-funded copycats? |
| **Regulatory capture** | Compliance requirement creates product need | Very strong | Does regulation mandate this, or merely make it useful? |
| **Workflow integration** | Product embedded in daily operations | Moderate-strong | How many integrations required? What does rip-out cost? |
| **Brand/trust** | Established credibility in regulated domain | Weak for startups | How long to build in this sector? Who vouches for you? |

### 3. Competitive Response Analysis
For each major incumbent, ask:
- **Build:** Could they replicate the core feature set? At what cost? In what timeframe?
- **Buy:** Would they acquire this company? At what stage? What's the walk-away price?
- **Partner:** Would they white-label or co-develop? Under what terms?
- **Ignore:** Is the market segment too small or too peripheral to warrant their attention?
- **Compete on price:** Could they bundle this feature for free to existing customers?

### 4. Porter's Five Forces (Adapted for B2B SaaS)
- **Threat of new entrants:** Funding environment, technical barriers, data barriers, regulatory barriers
- **Bargaining power of buyers:** Enterprise concentration, switching costs, budget discretionary vs. mandatory
- **Bargaining power of suppliers:** Cloud providers, data licensors, key talent
- **Threat of substitutes:** Manual process, consulting, do-nothing, adjacent tools
- **Competitive rivalry:** Number of well-funded direct competitors, growth rate of market

### Structural Gap Validation Questions
Before claiming "no competitor does X":
1. Is X patentable or at least trade-secret defensible?
2. Why hasn't X been built? (technical infeasibility? no market? tried and failed?)
3. Which incumbents are one acquisition away from doing X?
4. Which well-funded startups have raised but not yet announced their product?

## Market Size Methodology

### TAM / SAM / SOM Distinction
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** The theoretical max if you captured 100% of the global opportunity
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** The portion your product can actually serve given current geography, language, feature set
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** What you can realistically win in years 1–3 given your sales capacity and competitive dynamics

**Red flag:** Plans that cite TAM figures to justify SOM projections. A $50B TAM does not mean a $500M SOM is achievable. 

### Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down
- **Top-down** (market report × penetration rate): useful for investor narrative, not for operating plan
- **Bottom-up** (number of buyers × ACV × conversion rate): required for financial model validity

Always require a bottom-up model. Always challenge the conversion rate assumption.

## Competitive Position Summary Template

When summarizing competitive position, use this structure:

```
Competitive Position: [Differentiated / Parity / Behind]
Primary moat: [Type and strength assessment]
Key threat: [Most likely competitive response from which incumbent]
Time to competitive parity by incumbent: [Estimate with rationale]
Structural gap validity: [Confirmed / Questionable / Likely false]
```
