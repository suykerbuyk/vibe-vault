# Funding Sources and Investment Pathways — Reference

## Framework: Non-Dilutive First, Equity Second

Early-stage companies should exhaust non-dilutive funding before equity to preserve cap table.
Priority order: grants → tax credits → revenue-based → equity.

---

## Non-Dilutive Government Funding (United States)

### SBIR / STTR Programs
The primary federal innovation funding program. Available across 11 federal agencies.

| Phase | Award Range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Phase I | $50K–$300K | Feasibility / proof of concept (6 months) |
| Phase II | $400K–$2M | Full R&D, prototype development (2 years) |
| Phase IIB / SBIR Bridge | Varies | Commercialization bridge |

**Most relevant agencies for RegTech / knowledge graph / AI:**
- **NSF SBIR** — Technology commercialization, AI, data platforms; accepts pre-revenue companies
- **DARPA** — Defense-adjacent applications (CMMC compliance, DoD procurement graph)
- **DHS SBIR** — Homeland security, regulatory compliance infrastructure
- **Treasury / FinCEN** — Financial regulatory technology (limited SBIR, but BAA opportunities)
- **DOJ / FBI** — Compliance and enforcement intelligence tools

**Key requirements:** Company must be < 500 employees, US-owned, majority US-operated R&D.

**STTR difference:** Requires formal partnership with a research institution (university or federal lab). Useful if academic validation is part of the strategy.

**Application cadence:** NSF SBIR has rolling deadlines; most agencies have 2–3 solicitation windows/year.

### NIST Programs
- **NIST MEP (Manufacturing Extension Partnership):** Relevant if supply chain compliance vertical is pursued
- **NIST NCCoE:** Cybersecurity-adjacent; relevant for CMMC compliance graph use case
- **NIST SBIR:** Technology standards and measurement; relevant for schema standardization work

### DoD-Specific Pathways
For plans with defense/procurement or CMMC compliance applications:
- **DoD SBIR/STTR:** Largest single SBIR funder by volume
- **DIU (Defense Innovation Unit):** Rapid prototype agreements, not grants; requires demonstrated product
- **OTA (Other Transaction Authority):** Flexible contracting for non-traditional defense contractors
- **AFWERX / NavalX / ArmyFutures:** Service-branch innovation programs with SBIR-adjacent funding

### DOE Programs (If Energy Regulation Vertical)
- **ARPA-E:** Advanced energy research; relevant if energy regulation compliance is a vertical
- **DOE SBIR:** Energy regulatory compliance, grid standards

## Non-Dilutive Funding (International)

### European Union
- **EU Horizon Europe (EIC Accelerator):** Up to €2.5M grant + €15M equity for deep tech; requires EU nexus
- **EU Horizon Europe (EIC Pathfinder):** Exploratory research; relevant for academic validation track
- **Digital Europe Programme:** Data governance and AI deployment in the EU
- **Innovate UK (UK):** Up to £3M for R&D-intensive companies; Brexit-independent track available

### Sector-Specific International
- **ESMA FinTech:** European Securities and Markets Authority innovation programs
- **FCA Regulatory Sandbox (UK):** Not funding, but regulatory safe harbor for testing
- **MAS FinTech (Singapore):** Monetary Authority of Singapore; relevant for APAC regulatory graph

## Tax Incentives

### US Federal
- **R&D Tax Credit (Section 41):** 20% credit on qualified research expenses above a base amount. For startups with no tax liability: can offset payroll taxes (up to $500K/year under CHIPS Act expansion). This is real money for pre-revenue companies. Qualified activities include: developing novel algorithms, database architecture, schema design, and software for new functionality.
- **Section 1202 QSBS:** Investors in qualified small business stock may exclude up to $10M in capital gains. This is a meaningful investor incentive to include in fundraising materials.
- **Opportunity Zone Investment:** If operating in a designated OZ, investors receive capital gains deferral. Less relevant for pure software, but worth noting if physical presence matters.

### State-Level
- **Colorado (relevant given Loveland, CO location):**
  - Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Grant: Up to $250K matching grants for advanced tech companies
  - Colorado Office of Economic Development (OEDIT): Various programs for tech companies
  - Colorado R&D Credit: State-level complement to federal R&D credit
- **General state R&D credits:** ~35 states offer some form; worth reviewing for the state of incorporation and primary operations

## Equity Investment Pathways

### Pre-Seed / Seed
**Typical check size:** $500K–$3M  
**Relevant investor types:**
- **Generalist seed funds** (500 Startups, Y Combinator, TechStars — legal/RegTech track)
- **Legal tech focused:** Laika, Ironclad, LexCheck investors; Legaltech Fund; Bessemer (legal tech portfolio)
- **RegTech focused:** ING Ventures (RegTech focus), Illuminate Financial, Jungle Ventures (APAC)
- **FinTech crossover:** QED Investors, Ribbit Capital (compliance-heavy FinTech)
- **Deep tech / graph:** First Round Capital, Index Ventures (graph/AI)

### Series A
**Typical check size:** $5M–$20M  
**Milestone required:** $500K–$2M ARR, 5–15 enterprise customers, clear ICP validation  
**Relevant funds:**
- Accel, Andreessen Horowitz (enterprise SaaS)
- Insight Partners, Vista (B2B SaaS at scale)
- Regulatory-specific: ICONIQ, General Atlantic (compliance platforms)

### Strategic / Corporate Investment
High-value for RegTech because it provides:
- Customer reference + distribution
- Domain credibility
- Potential acquisition pathway

**Candidates for a regulatory knowledge graph:**
- **CUBE Global:** Already in regulatory knowledge graph; more likely acquirer than investor
- **Wolters Kluwer Ventures:** Active corporate VC in RegTech
- **Thomson Reuters Ventures:** Active in legal tech and regulatory AI
- **LexisNexis / RELX:** Active acquirer in legal/regulatory data
- **Microsoft (M12):** Active in enterprise software; has CUBE partnership interest
- **Salesforce Ventures:** Platform investment; relevant if legal workflow integration is pursued
- **ServiceNow:** Has GRC module; could invest or acquire to extend

### Government / Institutional Investors
For plans with strong public-sector or SupTech (supervisory tech) applications:
- **In-Q-Tel (IQT):** CIA/IC-affiliated strategic investor; relevant for defense/intelligence compliance applications
- **AFWERX Spark:** DoD-affiliated; investment + contract pathway
- **NATO DIANA:** Defense and security deep tech accelerator

## Customer-Funded Development

Often underutilized by early-stage B2B companies:

- **Paid pilot programs:** Large enterprises will often fund a $50K–$250K pilot to evaluate a novel capability. This is non-dilutive revenue and a proof point.
- **Co-development agreements:** A buyer with a specific pain point co-funds development in exchange for pricing preferences or licensing rights. Common in financial services.
- **Government procurement (FAR Part 12):** Commercial products can be procured by federal agencies without extensive acquisition overhead. A GSA Schedule registration ($5K–$20K to obtain) opens federal customer revenue.

## Funding Strategy by Stage

| Stage | Primary Path | Secondary Path | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-MVP | NSF SBIR Phase I + bootstrap | Angel round | VC (too early) |
| MVP complete | SBIR Phase II + seed | Strategic pilot agreements | Large equity round (dilutive before proof) |
| First revenue | Seed equity + R&D tax credits | Series A prep | Venture debt (too early without revenue durability) |
| $1M+ ARR | Series A + QSBS investor incentive | Strategic investment | Government grants (diminishing returns vs. opportunity cost) |
